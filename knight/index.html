What is a Food Recall?

A food recall is an action taken to remove a product from the market due to safety concerns, often prompted by contamination, mislabeling, or allergen risks. Recalls can be initiated by manufacturers voluntarily or mandated by government agencies when a significant health risk is identified. The goal is to protect consumers from potentially harmful products and prevent foodborne illnesses.

Why are they dangerous?

Food recalls can be dangerous because they often involve products that may be contaminated with harmful pathogens, such as bacteria or viruses, which can lead to serious health issues. Consuming contaminated food can result in foodborne illnesses, causing symptoms ranging from mild gastrointestinal discomfort to severe complications requiring hospitalization.
